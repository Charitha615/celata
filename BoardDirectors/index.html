Thareendra Kalpage
Member,Board of Directors

Thareendra Kalpage (born June 26, 1981) is a Sri Lankan-Asian entrepreneur and business executive who co-founded Digital Zyndicate Co., a venture capital firm based in Denver, Colorado, and Meetanu, a software firm involved in the hospitality industry. He also invested in several notable ventures, including the Duthaya Digital Services who owns and operates the Duthaya mobile app, which is currently revolutionizing the consumer market as an online and mobile marketplace that connects freelance labor (messengers) with local demand (end users). Thareendra also co-owns a premium leather bag range with a leading US retailer. He began his entrepreneurial journey after nearly 20 years of experience working in senior leadership roles, including 7 years where we started 2015 as the Chief Operating Officer and then, beginning in 2018, promoted as the Chief Executive Officer of Epic Lanka Technologies, which under Mr. Kalpage grew to become one of the country's leading and most innovative software firms, with an absolute market share on eGovernment initiatives in Sri Lanka. At the same time, Under Mr. Kalpage’s leadership Epic Lanka expanded their business into many new sectors with massive projects that can be considered their own startups, such as a collaboration with the Development Lotteries Board of Sri Lanka to launch South East Asia's First Ever Mobile Lottery App, a joint venture with Sri Lanka Telecom to launch HelaViru Digital Market Place facilitated to Digitalize Agriculture Value Chain, CIPE and Life Hardware product line, which was the company's entry into Hardware Manufacturing Before joining Epic Lanka, he worked for Microsoft Sri Lanka for over 5 years as a key player in strategizing and shaping Microsoft's commercial engagement with the Sri Lankan government. Mr. Kalpage led Microsoft Sri Lanka's Public Sector Account Team to some ground-breaking achievements from a Microsoft standpoint, including the signing of the first ever Microsoft enterprise agreement with a Government Owned Entity, and having Microsoft technology selected as a preferred technology stack for Treasury Automation and Inland Revenue Automations, which is still one of the largest government procurements related to Information Technology. Mr. Kalpage led the adoption of Microsoft's products into government-owned/managed institutes, and during his tenure, he increased Microsoft adoption in government by multiples, which was unprecedented at the time. Mr. Kalpage also worked for Sierra Information Technologies and the DSI Samson Group, where he began his career and made some notable and significant contributions as a young professional, including one of his proposals to management to start their own Information Technology Company. He wrote the first business proposal on the subject, which sparked the formation of Samson Information Technologies, which later became DTech. Mr. Kalpage is also a well-known writer on economics, technology, and global politics, and his articles have received widespread attention. He is also a prominent figure in assisting young founders, where he continues to assist them in realizing their vision and creating economic opportunities. He also advises on key strategic initiatives and mentors and coaches the leadership teams of companies in which he is an investor. Thareendra is interested in investing, coaching, and sharing his knowledge with entrepreneurs in addition to his ongoing work. Mr. Kalpage prefers to identify as a business leader who understands value creation in the context of the ecosystem, which includes the tech, economy, science, social, and geopolitical landscape. Mr. Kalpage holds LLB (Hons) Law from Buckinghamshire New University, and attended the Senior Management Programme at Lee Kuan Yew School of Public Policy as well holds certification on Big Data and Cloud Computing Technologies from Yonsei University.
